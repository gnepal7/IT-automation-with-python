# Recognizing burnout
 . Career burnout, Burnout can occur from many things, including::
  - Unclear purpose
  - Overly demanding schedules
  - Unclear expectations

 . There are many common signs of burnout: 
   - Constantly exhausted
   - Physical pain, such as migraines, headaches, and muscle aches and pains
   - Changes in appetite
   - Lack of energy and interest in activities outside of work
   - Sleeping more or less than usual

 . Identifying burnout triggers
  - Lack of agency causes burnout: no control or progress.
  - Poor sleep reduces focus and increases burnout risk.
  - Lack of community or purpose leads to burnout.
 
 . Repairing causes of burnout
   - Reframe thoughts about your situation to reduce burnout.
   - Seek help from HR, supervisors, or colleagues.
   - Reset expectations by comparing deliverables with personal goals.
 . Sustainable workplaces
  - Schedule flexibility supports life events and reduces stress.
  - Mental health resources include paid time off for rest.
  - Manageable workloads promote long-term health and career growth.
